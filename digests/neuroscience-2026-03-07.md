🧠 *Neuroscience Digest — March 7, 2026*

**1. Brain Prize 2026: Decoding Touch & Pain**
The world's largest brain research prize (€1M) went to David Ginty (Harvard) & Patrik Ernfors (Karolinska) for mapping how nerve cells transform touch, heat, and pain into neural signals — rewriting textbook somatosensation and laying groundwork for targeted pain therapies.
→ [BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/the-worlds-largest-brain-research-prize-awarded-for-groundbreaking-discoveries-on-how-we-sense-touch-and-pain)

**2. BCI Translation Era: From Lab to Clinic**
Brain-computer interfaces hit a turning point in 2026 — real-time speech neuroprosthetics are repeatable, FDA engagement is serious, and Neuralink/Synchron are scaling trials internationally. The question is no longer "can it work?" but "can it work daily, reliably, in real lives?"
→ [The Neurotech Newsletter](https://theneurotechnewsletter.substack.com/p/braincomputer-interfaces-in-2026)

**3. Stanford AI Turns Brain Scans Into Thought "Movies"**
Stanford HAI researchers unveiled an AI system that converts messy brain scans into real-time visual "movies" of thoughts propagating across the brain — with potential to transform diagnosis of depression, tumors, and other neurological conditions.
→ [@StanfordHAI on X](https://x.com/StanfordHAI/status/2029799640325910663)

**4. Parkinson's Stem Cell Trial: Restoring Dopamine**
A breakthrough clinical trial is testing engineered stem cells that prompt the brain to restore its own dopamine production in Parkinson's patients — targeting the root cause rather than masking symptoms.
→ [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/mind_brain/neuroscience/)

**5. Exercise Repairs the Aging Blood-Brain Barrier**
New research shows physical activity triggers the liver to release an enzyme that clears a harmful protein responsible for making the blood-brain barrier leaky with age — providing a molecular mechanism linking exercise to sharper cognition.
→ [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/mind_brain/neuroscience/)

**6. EEG Maps Prospective Memory Gaps in Parkinson's**
A high-density EEG study published in *Frontiers in Neuroscience* pinpoints the neural correlates of prospective memory deficits (forgetting to do things) in Parkinson's disease — advancing understanding of cognitive decline beyond motor symptoms.
→ [@FrontNeurosci on X](https://x.com/FrontNeurosci/status/2029944997404103018)
